## Lessons reconfirmed (already in CLAUDE.md)
1. **Unsplash is deep for famous landmarks** (Parliament, Big Ben,
   Trafalgar, the Abbey, the Barbican) but returns wrong subjects for
   restricted-access / interior-famous places. For those — Westminster
   Hall's hammerbeam roof, the Banqueting House Rubens ceiling, the
   Churchill War Rooms Map Room, the interior-famous City churches — the
   **owner pastes a photo into chat**; pull it from
   `/root/.claude/projects/-home-user-TRAVEL-GUIDED-TOUR/<session>.jsonl`
   (base64 image blocks), decode with Pillow, crop, upload.
2. **Verify gate caught many look-alikes** this round: the V&A (vs
   National Gallery), Budapest's parliament (vs Parliament Square), the
   Women-of-WWII and Battle-of-Britain memorials (vs the Cenotaph), the
   Cathedral of St Paul in Minnesota / NZ churches (earlier), a
   wood-panelled regional war museum (vs the Churchill War Rooms). Always
   eyeball the pixels; never trust the search query match.
3. **EXIF:** `ImageOps.exif_transpose()` then NO manual rotate.
4. **Tall subjects** (columns/spires/towers/church fronts) get a
   top-biased crop since the 1200×900 hero is landscape.
5. **Unsplash free tier ≈ 50 searches/hr** — a few queries 403'd late in
   the batch. Pace the searches or fall back to CC0 for a tour.
6. **Slow CI:** the iOS-simulator job's sim-prep step ran 1–5 min, pushing
   the unit-test job to ~6–13 min wall-clock; the MCP `get_check_runs`
   read also lags. Confirm true status with
   `actions_get get_workflow_job` before deciding a job is stuck.
