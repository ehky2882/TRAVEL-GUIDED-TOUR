## The big change — rails drawer (PR #194)

The drawer body now renders `HomeRailsViewModel.rails()` (built in
session 19's PR #131 era, unused until now) instead of the flat
in-view list:

- **Stack:** compact "Continue listening" row → **NEAR YOU** →
  **IN VIEW** (only when panned ≥500m from the user) → one shelf per
  category, whole catalog, capped 10/rail.
- **Category rails sort by distance from the viewer** (user location,
  falling back to map center) — new `viewerLocation` helper.
- **Chips are jump-scroll now, not filters** — selecting a category
  glides the rails to that shelf via `ScrollViewReader` (opens the
  drawer from peek first). "All" returns to top.
- **Peek header** still shows "N TOURS IN VIEW" (map-scoped count);
  large shows "LET'S EXPLORE TOGETHER!".

### Rail card design (owner-iterated, 4 rounds at the sim)

- **260pt wide, 4:3 hero (260×195)** — exactly the catalog's 1200×900
  gallery aspect, so heroes render uncropped. One dominant card +
  ~78pt peek of the next; the next rail's title peeks from the bottom.
  (Tried: 220×140, 180 square, 160 square, 280×210 — owner picked
  260/4:3.)
- Titles **BODY all-caps, one line, tail ellipsis**; subtitle = maker
  name (no "by"); duration matches subtitle color (secondaryText);
  bookmark affordance on the hero corner (parity with the old list
  card).
- Rail title row: SF Mono ALL-CAPS caption + trailing chevron.
- **Rail horizontal padding sits on the ScrollView viewport**, not the
  content — mid-scroll, cards clip at the drawer margins instead of
  sliding edge-to-edge (owner request).

### Continue-listening row

- Sources: **(1) tour currently loaded in the player** (same signal as
  the mini-player: `audioPlayer.state != .idle` + `currentSourceId`),
  **(2) fallback: most-recent unfinished library entry by
  `lastListenedAt`** (was savedAt — a bug the owner had flagged).
- "Recently viewed" row **removed** (owner trial; still in Library).
- Square corners; leading inset = vertical inset (8pt); all-caps title.

## Detent persistence fix (the "flash" bug)

Exiting a tour detail used to flash the drawer back in after the
slide-down. Cause: ContentView removed the whole drawer while ANY
detail was registered (`navState.isShowingDetail`), and the detail's
`pop()` fires only after the dismiss animation. Fix:

- New `tourLayerCoversDrawer` @State in ContentView — set when the
  tour layer presents **from the Home root** (`pushedDepth == 0` at
  present time), cleared by a new **completion param on
  `BottomLayerController.dismiss`** when the slide finishes.
- While true, the drawer **stays mounted underneath the layer** — the
  dismiss reveals it sitting at its old detent. Tours opened from
  inside Search still hide the drawer (depth ≥ 1 at present).
- `BottomSheet.swift` itself untouched (detents/drag/geometry settled).

## Map ergonomics

- **Compass relocated:** removed `MapCompass()` from `.mapControls`
  (framework pins it top-trailing, hidden under search bar + chips);
  `Map(scope:)` + `@Namespace mapScope` in HomeView lets a manual
  `MapCompass(scope:)` sit **trailing edge, bottom-aligned with the
  recenter button** (same bottom-padding formula as the control
  stack). MapKit auto-visibility preserved (shows only when rotated
  off north). NOT hidden while the map moves — rotation happens
  mid-gesture. ⚠️ **Needs a hand check:** sim automation can't rotate
  (⌥-drag in Simulator.app works manually).
- **44pt pin hit areas:** pins/clusters draw 16–34pt but hit-test at
  Apple's 44pt minimum (`.frame(44).contentShape(Circle())` wrapper in
  `pinView`). Owner's "pins feel difficult to tap" — root cause was a
  16pt target.

## Owner's design rationale (worth keeping)

Owner asked for my honest rails-vs-list take mid-session; the agreed
framing: rails win at 243 tours (the flat list was an unrankable wall
of 85 cards), but the **map↔drawer coupling is Atlas's identity** —
keep "In view"/"Near you" prominent, don't let the drawer become a
generic catalog. The `>` chevron on rail headers is the natural hook
for a future per-rail "see all" vertical list (old `TourListCard`
could return there).
