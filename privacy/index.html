Atlas Privacy Policy

Effective May 19, 2026 · Last updated May 19, 2026

Atlas is an audio-tour app for places you walk through. This policy explains, in plain language, what data Atlas does and does not collect about you, and what it does with it.

The short version. Atlas does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information off your device. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, no tracking. Location is used only on your device to play the right audio at the right place.

What Atlas accesses on your device

Location

Atlas asks for your device's location to do two things:

  • Sort tours by distance from where you are, so the most relevant tours appear first.
  • Detect when you arrive at a stop in a multi-stop tour and automatically play that stop's audio (for tours that opt into automatic playback).

Your location is used only on your device. Atlas never sends it to us, our servers, or anyone else. You can revoke location permission at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Atlas. If location is denied, tours are still browsable and playable; only the location-based sorting and automatic-stop playback are unavailable.

Local notifications

Atlas can show a notification when you arrive at a stop in a tour you're listening to. These notifications are generated locally by your device — nothing leaves the device to produce them. You can disable notifications at any time in iOS Settings.

What Atlas stores on your device

  • Tours you've saved or downloaded for offline listening.
  • Your listening progress within each tour.
  • Your recent searches and recently viewed tours.
  • Audio files for any tours you've downloaded for offline listening.

All of this is stored locally on your device and never transmitted. Uninstalling Atlas removes it.

What Atlas does not collect

  • No name, email, phone number, or other identifying information.
  • No account or sign-in — Atlas has no user accounts in V1.
  • No analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or usage tracking. We do not know who you are or how you use the app.
  • No advertising identifiers or ad tracking.
  • No payment information — all V1 content is free.
  • No access to your contacts, calendar, microphone, or camera.

Network requests

Atlas makes network requests to download audio files and tour images from a content-delivery network. These requests are simple HTTPS retrievals — no identifiers, account info, or location data are attached. Standard HTTPS metadata (your IP address and the time of the request) is visible to the hosting provider, as is true of every website you visit, but is not collected, stored, or aggregated by Atlas.

Third parties

Atlas does not use any third-party analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, or data brokers. The only third party involved in V1 is the audio-hosting provider that serves audio files, which only receives plain HTTPS requests for files.

Children's privacy

Atlas is suitable for all ages and does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If Atlas's data practices ever change — for example, if a future release adds optional accounts or analytics — this policy will be updated and the "Last updated" date at the top will reflect the change. Significant changes will also be noted in the app's release notes.
