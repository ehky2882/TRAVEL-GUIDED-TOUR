About a decade ago, vohg magazine ranked the street you're standing on the second-coolest neighbourhood on earth, behind one in Tokyo. Try not to hold it against the place.

You're on Queen Street West, west of Bathurst, in the stretch that got relabelled West Queen West once plain Queen West stopped sounding far enough out. Look at the mix along here: a boutique hotel, a vintage shop, a gallery, a cafe charging real money for coffee, and, somewhere in the gaps, the older, cheaper businesses that haven't been priced out yet.

That tension is the whole story of this street, and it moves in one direction: west. For decades, Toronto's cheap rent and its artists kept migrating together, one stretch of Queen at a time. The galleries and studios would land somewhere affordable and make it interesting; the interesting would draw the boutiques; the boutiques would lift the rent; and the artists would pack up and move a few blocks further west to do it all again. Queen West became West Queen West became Parkdale.

The two grand old hotels along here, the Drake and the Gladstone, are the markers of the turn — both faded flophouses reborn as design hotels right about when the magazines started paying attention.

None of this is unique to Toronto, and it isn't simply good or bad. Something is gained — the street is genuinely alive — and something is lost every time the rent resets. Reasonable people who live here argue about it constantly.

So enjoy the version you're seeing today, because it's temporary by design. Somewhere a few blocks west, on a cheaper corner you'd walk past without noticing, the next Queen West is quietly being invented right now.
