You can smell this place before you can see it — coffee, incense, fish on ice, someone's lunch three doors down.

Stand somewhere near the corner of Augusta Avenue and Baldwin Street, with the narrow shopfronts pressing in on both sides and the produce spilling out onto the sidewalk in crates. Look at the buildings themselves for a second, underneath the murals and the hand-painted signs. They're just houses. Small Victorian workers' cottages, built in the 1880s for Irish and Scottish labourers, with the front rooms knocked out and turned into shops.

That's the whole trick of Kensington, and it's worth knowing before you wander in. Nobody ever really built this market. It just kept being handed down.

The first wave were Eastern European Jewish families, in the early 1900s, selling from stalls and front stoops — a couple of their synagogues are still tucked into the side streets. Then Portuguese and Italian families. Then Caribbean, then Vietnamese, then Latin American, then East African. Each one arrived, set up shop, and painted over the last one's sign without quite erasing it. You're standing in the layers right now.

Here's the part that keeps it honest. These streets are narrow — too narrow for a proper delivery truck, nearly impossible to park on. That's not a charming accident; it's the reason the chain stores never moved in, and the reason the rents stayed within reach of someone selling empanadas out of a former living room. The awkwardness is the whole defence.

It nearly didn't survive. In the 1960s, a city plan proposed levelling these little houses for apartment towers — the same idea that flattened other neighbourhoods downtown. Kensington said no, and in 2006 the country made it official, naming the whole market a National Historic Site — not for any one building, but for being a living record of who kept arriving.

So take your time. Buy something small from someone who clearly made it themselves. Most cities replace themselves — knock the old thing down, put the new thing up. Kensington just kept adding rooms.
