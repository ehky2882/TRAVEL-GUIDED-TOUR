Fair warning before anything else: this walk is a climb. A real one — a few hundred stairs, a steady uphill on gravel switchbacks, forty-five minutes to an hour going up depending on your legs, and a couple of hours for the round trip. Bring water, wear shoes that mean it, and in winter, boots with proper grip. It isn't a stroll with a view at the end; the climb is the point, and I'll explain why as we go.

Because here's the thing about this mountain: people have been climbing it on purpose for nearly four centuries. The very first ascent that history remembers was made in the winter of sixteen forty-three by may zoh nuv himself — the founder you may have met in bronze down at plahs darm — and he did it carrying something heavy. Hold that thought until the top. Every walk up Mount Royal since has been, knowingly or not, a repeat of his.

Start at Peel may troh station and walk up Peel Street — uphill already, consider it your warm-up — until the street ends at ah vuh noo day pan and the green wall of the mountain rises in front of you. That's the park entrance, and that's where I'll pick you up.
