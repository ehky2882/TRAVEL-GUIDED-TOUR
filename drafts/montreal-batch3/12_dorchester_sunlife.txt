The most valuable basement in the history of the British Empire sits under the building on the east side of this square.

You're in Dorchester Square, a green rectangle in the middle of downtown, and the building to look at is the one that resembles a granite mountain range: the Sun Life Building, rising in tiers behind four-storey Corinthian columns. When its final phase was finished in 1931, it was the largest building by floor space anywhere in the British Empire — an insurance company's headquarters built with the confidence of a state.

Which is roughly how it was used. In the summer of 1940, with Britain facing invasion, crates began arriving in Montreal marked with one word: fish. Inside was a staggering share of Britain's negotiable wealth — securities shipped in secret across a U-boat-hunted Atlantic — and it went into a vault built in a hurry three storeys beneath this building, guarded around the clock by the Mounties. From that basement, a secret office quietly sold Britain's holdings on the New York Stock Exchange for years to pay for the war. The building's five thousand employees never knew. And the rumour you may have heard — that the Crown Jewels were down there too — was a legend, but a manufactured one: the story was deliberately spread around Montreal to explain all the unusual activity. The cover story outlived the secret.

The square has a buried history of its own, and an older one. Before it was a park, this ground was a Catholic cemetery, filled during the cholera epidemics of the nineteenth century; most of the graves were moved to the mountain in the 1850s, though not all, and the city has walked gently over the rest ever since.

So take the square's measure. A park over a cemetery, facing a vault that once held an empire's fortune, in a downtown that streams past on its way to somewhere louder. The most consequential places here have never once asked for attention.

Find a bench for a minute before you go. Above you, the office floors hum along as they have for nearly a century — and every person inside is working, as it were, over the fish.
