Cities write their own biographies, and Montreal wrote its first chapters in a district you can cross in twenty minutes. This walk is that biography — five stops, about a kilometre and a half of flat, easy ground, from the square where the church once ran everything to the harbour where the money came in. The only physical demand is the cobblestone, which has opinions, so mind your footing and bring the comfortable shoes.

One note before we start: the story won't arrive entirely in order. We begin in the middle, at plahs darm, because that's where the city's long argument with itself is easiest to see — then we drop back to the very beginning, to the ground Montreal was founded on, and work our way forward through markets and parliaments until we reach the water. Cities remember this way too, out of sequence, one landmark at a time.

So find plahs darm — the square in front of noh truh dahm basilica, with a monument at its centre and the great stone banks standing around it like they own the place. Which, for a long stretch of this city's history, they more or less did. That's stop one. I'll meet you by the monument.
