You're looking up at the Tour Montparnasse — a single, stark, dark skyscraper rising more than two hundred metres straight out of a city of low, pale, uniform rooftops. It looks completely out of place, and that is the most important thing about it, because Parisians have never quite forgiven it.

When it went up in the early nineteen seventies, it was meant to signal a modern, vertical future for the city. Instead, the reaction was horror. The lone black tower looming over the tender old skyline was so widely disliked that, just a couple of years after it was finished, the city effectively banned any more skyscrapers in the historic centre. So the Tour Montparnasse stands almost alone — not because no one else wanted to build tall, but because this one building made the very idea unthinkable. It's a monument to its own mistake.

But here's the joke, and it's a good one. Go up to the observation deck at the top, and you get what may be the finest panoramic view in all of Paris — the whole city laid out beneath you, the Eiffel Tower standing proud in the middle distance, the river curling through it all. And the reason it's the best view in the city is gloriously simple: it's the one high place in Paris from which you cannot see the Tour Montparnasse — because you're standing on it.

That's the line every Parisian will tell you with a perfectly straight face: the best view in Paris is from the top of the Tour Montparnasse, because it's the only spot in the city where the Tour Montparnasse doesn't spoil it.

So let the unloved building do you a favour. Ride to the top, turn toward the Eiffel Tower, and enjoy the one view of Paris that the tower beneath your feet can't ruin.
