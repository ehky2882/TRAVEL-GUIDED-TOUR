You're at the California Academy of Sciences, directly across the Music Concourse from the de Young. From the front it looks low and modern and fairly calm — but the whole trick of this building is on top, where you can't quite see it from here.

The roof is alive. It's a two-and-a-half-acre living roof, planted with native California wildflowers and grasses, rolling in seven green hills that echo the hills of San Francisco itself. It insulates the building, soaks up rainwater, and gives native bugs and birds a home seventy feet up. The architect, Renzo Piano, basically lifted a piece of the California landscape and draped it over a museum.

And what's under that roof is the real wonder: this is four institutions in one. A natural history museum, an aquarium, a planetarium, and a four-story indoor rainforest under a glass dome, all beneath a single roof. You can stand in a rainforest, ride down to a coral reef, and walk out under a model of the universe, without ever stepping outside. There's even an albino alligator, which the city has a strange amount of affection for.

That's the idea this whole park keeps repeating, in one building: the natural world, gathered up and handed to the public, free to wander into. A roof that's a meadow, over a museum that's a planet.
