Fair warning: Hollywood Boulevard is the most famous street in Los Angeles and the one most likely to divide a carload of visitors. It's glorious and scrappy at the same time — movie palaces and souvenir shops, history and hustle, shoulder to shoulder. The trick is knowing what you're looking at, because this boulevard isn't just where fame is celebrated. It's where fame — the modern, manufactured, red-carpet kind — was invented, and this walk visits the factory floor.

Five stops, about a mile, flat and linear the whole way, which by Los Angeles standards makes it practically a stroll. You'll start with the product line itself — the brass and tuh rat soh stars underfoot. Then the temple where the stars leave their handprints, the theater where they collect their Oscars, the older palace up the block where the whole premiere ritual was staged for the very first time in nineteen twenty two, and finally, around the corner on Vine, a tower that broadcasts the town's name to the sky in Morse code — which will make sense when you get there.

One honest note before we start: the boulevard is partway through a years-long effort to spruce itself up, so expect some construction alongside the glamour. Don't let it throw you. Hollywood has never once finished selling itself.

Start at the corner of Hollywood Boulevard and lah bray uh Avenue, the western end of the Walk of Fame, facing east. The stars begin at your feet.
