Most city walks show you buildings. This one shows you a way of living — because the plah toh and Mile End conduct more of their private life in public than almost anywhere else on the continent, and once you learn to read the signs of it, you'll see it everywhere: on the staircases, on the balconies, in the squares, down the laneways, and finally in a line-up on a sidewalk that smells of wood smoke.

The practicalities are gentle. It's about three kilometres end to end, flat with the occasional soft rise, an hour or so of actual walking plus however long you linger — and you should linger; that's rather the point of the neighbourhood. Any season works, though this walk was born for a warm evening.

We start at square san loo ee. Take the may troh to Sherbrooke station and walk a block west, and you'll arrive at a small park of big trees, a cast-iron fountain, and some of the most photographed houses in the city. Find a spot near the fountain and we'll begin.
