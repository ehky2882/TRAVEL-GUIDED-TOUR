You're about to walk one street, and on it, three of the great art museums on earth. This is the Paseo del Prado, a wide, tree-lined boulevard running north to south through the centre of Madrid, and the stretch you'll walk is known as the golden triangle of art: the Prado, the Thyssen-Bornemisza, and the Reina Sofía — three collections within a few minutes of each other that between them cover roughly eight hundred years of painting.

The boulevard itself is the point as much as the museums. In the seventeen-hundreds, King Charles the Third — the king Madrid still half-jokingly calls its best mayor — set out to turn this avenue into a promenade of science and learning, lined with fountains, a botanical garden, and what is now the Prado. It worked so well that in 2021 the whole axis, boulevard and park together, was named a World Heritage Site, under the lovely title "the Landscape of Light."

A word of honesty before we start. You cannot do all three of these museums in one day and still enjoy them. Treat this walk as the thread that strings them together: do the fountains and the buildings now, and pick one museum to go inside, or come back another day. It's flat, it's straight, and it's about twenty minutes end to end without stopping.

We'll go north to south. Start at the grand fountain at the top of the boulevard, the one everyone photographs: the Fuente de Cibeles.
