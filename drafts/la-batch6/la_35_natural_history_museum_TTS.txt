NATURAL HISTORY MUSEUM OF LOS ANGELES COUNTY

People love to say Los Angeles has no history. The city's answer is this building, which holds around thirty-five million pieces of it.

You should be at the north end of Exposition Park, facing the Natural History Museum — the grand brick-and-marble building whose domed rotunda rises behind the rose garden. It opened in nineteen thirteen, which makes it one of the oldest museum buildings in the city, and the age matters more than it sounds.

Consider what Los Angeles was in nineteen thirteen: no film industry to speak of, no freeways, and until November of that very year — when the aqueduct opened — no reliable water. The city built itself a serious museum before it built almost everything it's now famous for. That was the point. The boosters selling Los Angeles to the world needed proof it was a real city and not a land scheme with good weather, and a domed marble museum is exactly the kind of proof they meant.

It even hedged its bets on the name, opening as the Museum of History, Science and Art — three museums in one, because nobody yet knew what the young city would need. Art eventually moved out and got its own institutions; history and science stayed, which is why this is still the place where Los Angeles keeps its dinosaurs and its own civic memory under one roof.

The dinosaurs are the headliners — the hall includes a growth series of tuh ran uh sawr us rex, from baby to adult, which is a rarer sight than a single skeleton — and the collections behind the galleries run to some thirty-five million specimens, the largest natural history museum in the western United States. Two details worth carrying out with you: its collection of marine mammal remains is the second largest anywhere, behind only the smith soh nee un — and a good number of the ice age fossils here came out of the ground a few miles north, at the tar pits on wil sher. This is one of the only museums on earth that excavates mammoths from the middle of its own city.

So take the old line about los angeles having no history and hold it up against the rotunda. The city has plenty. It just keeps most of it in drawers.
