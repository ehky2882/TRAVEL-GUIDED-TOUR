This walk is three miles of the most famous coastline in the world — and almost nothing on it is doing what it was built to do.

That's the thread. The pier ahead of you was built to carry a sewer pipe. The gym down the sand was a Depression make-work project. Venice itself was built as a high-minded cultural resort, and the street that closes this walk spent most of its life as a strip of repair shops. Every one of them became famous for something its builders never planned — which, on this particular stretch of coast, turns out to be the local specialty.

The route runs south from the Santa Monica Pier along the beachfront path to Venice — past the original Muscle Beach, down the boardwalk, then a couple of blocks inland to the Venice Canals and Abbot Kinney Boulevard. It's flat the whole way, on a continuous paved path shared with bikes and skaters, and you can walk it in about an hour of moving time or rent a bike and ride it. Fair warning: on a sunny weekend the path is a river of people, so give yourself the afternoon. There's no rail line along the sand — if you drove, you'll want to loop back or catch a ride from the far end.

One more thing before you start. Keep an eye out for the name Kinney as you go. You'll meet him properly at the end of the walk, but the truth is you'll be inside his imagination for most of it.
