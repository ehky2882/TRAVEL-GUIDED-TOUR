NORTH BEACH & THE CLIMB TO COIT — Walk Intro (TTS-safe)

First, a correction: there is no beach in North Beach.

There used to be — a real one, on the bay — but the city filled it in more than a century ago, and the name stuck to dry land the way names do. What's here instead is something better: the closest thing San Francisco has to a European village, an Italian neighborhood layered over a bohemian one, wrapped around the base of a steep green hill with a tower on top.

This walk has a shape to it, and it's worth knowing before you start. We begin at the bottom, in the flat heart of North Beach, where the Beat poets ran a bookstore that went to court for the right to publish, and where the Italian San Francisco of cafés and a twin-spired church still holds on. Then we climb — and I mean climb — up the side of Telegraph Hill, past hidden garden staircases where a flock of wild parrots actually lives, to coyt Tower at the summit and one of the great views in the city.

Here's the honest part. The climb to the top is a real one — steep streets, then steep stairs — and it's the whole point of the walk, the slow rise from the bohemian village to the lookout above it. If your knees would rather not, you have an easy out: the number thirty-nine bus runs from Washington Square right up to coyt Tower, so you can ride up and walk down instead. No shame in it. The parrots don't care how you arrive.

Our first stop is at the bottom, where it all starts. Find the corner of kohlumbus Avenue and Broadway, and look for a wedge-shaped bookstore with its name running down the side: City Lights. I'll meet you out front.
