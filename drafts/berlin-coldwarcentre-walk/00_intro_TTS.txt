Keep an eye on the ground today. Again and again on this walk, a double row of cobblestones will slide across your path — through asphalt, across tram tracks, straight over squares — with no regard for the streets around it. It marks where the Berlin Wall stood, and here in the centre of the city it ignores the street plan entirely, cutting across blocks mid-stride, because the line is older than almost everything now built around it. Downtown Berlin was rebuilt around a scar, and on this walk you'll watch the city and the scar negotiate, corner by corner.

The route runs about two kilometres, all of it level: from pots dahm er plats past the Topography of Terror to Checkpoint Charlie, finishing at zhahn dar men markt — which is to say, from the Cold War's emptiest ground, through its darkest, past its most theatrical, to the loveliest square Berlin owns. That last stop is not an accident. You'll want it by then.

Fair warning: the middle of this walk crosses hard history, and we'll treat it accordingly.

Start at pots dahm er plats — the es bahn, oo bahn and regional trains all stop right underneath it. Come up to street level, stand among the towers, and we'll begin with the square that died and came back.
