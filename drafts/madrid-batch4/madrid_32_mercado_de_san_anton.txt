Stand in the little square at the centre of Chueca and take the temperature of the place. Rainbow flags in windows, busy terraces, a crowd that looks comfortable in its own skin. This is the heart of LGBTQ+ Madrid — and that's a recent thing, won rather than given.

A generation ago this neighbourhood had a very different reputation: run-down, edgy, the kind of place guidebooks warned you off. From the nineteen eighties on, the city's gay community moved in, opened bars and businesses, and slowly turned a neglected quarter into one of the most confident and sought-after corners of Madrid. By the time the city hosts its enormous Pride celebrations each summer — among the largest in the world — these streets are the centre of it, packed shoulder to shoulder. A neighbourhood that was once somewhere to avoid is now somewhere a whole city comes to celebrate.

The square takes its name, by the way, from a composer — Federico Chueca, who wrote the light, popular Spanish operettas called zarzuelas. There's a nice fit there: a district known for performance and good humour, named after a man who wrote crowd-pleasers.

Now find the market — Mercado de San Antón. From the outside it's a plain modern box, easy to overlook, but it tells the neighbourhood's whole story in three floors. The ground floor still works as a proper food market, the fishmongers and grocers locals actually use. Climb up and it changes: stalls to eat at, then a rooftop terrace with a bar and a view over the rooftops. The old neighbourhood market and the new social one, stacked on top of each other in the same building.

So this is a stop about reinvention, and it's worth saying plainly because it didn't have to happen. Streets get written off; communities get pushed to the margins. Here a marginal neighbourhood and a marginalised community found each other and built something open and warm out of it. Grab a coffee on a terrace, watch the square go by, and notice how thoroughly it feels like it was always meant to be this way. It wasn't. That's the point.
