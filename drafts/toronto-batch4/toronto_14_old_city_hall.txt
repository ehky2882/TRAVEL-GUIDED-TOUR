Look closely at the faces carved over the front entrance, because at least one of them is an insult, set in stone, on purpose.

You should be standing on Queen Street West, looking up at the great sandstone building across from the modern City Hall — a massive, rough-walled pile with a single off-centre clock tower rising high above the head of Bay Street. This is Old City Hall, and it was built by an architect with a grudge.

Start with the tower, then come down to the door.

When it opened in 1899, this was the largest civic building in North America, and that clock tower was the tallest structure in Canada for nearly two decades. The man behind it was E.J. Lennox, and he spent more than a decade wrangling with the city over budgets, materials and credit. When the building was finished, the council refused to put up a plaque naming him as the architect. So Lennox took his revenge in stone: he had his own name and the word "architect" carved into the frieze running around all four sides of the building, where you can still find it.

Then there are the faces. Above the entrance arches sits a row of small grotesque carvings — gargoyle-like heads — and the long-told story is that they're caricatures of the councillors Lennox had been fighting with. There's one face carved with a little more dignity than the rest. It's said to be Lennox himself, complete with his handlebar moustache, watching the others squirm for eternity.

The building served as the city's seat of government, then for decades as a courthouse. The courts moved out in 2025, and the city is now deciding what its next life should be.

[beat]

It nearly didn't survive at all. When the new City Hall opened across the street, there were serious plans to tear this one down — and the developers of a shopping mall a few blocks east wanted it gone too. A group of citizens fought to keep it. So the petty, magnificent monument that one stubborn architect built to spite his bosses is still here, outlasting every councillor whose face he mocked. Walk up close and find his signature. He earned it.
