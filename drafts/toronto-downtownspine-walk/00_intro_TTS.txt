Stand for a second at the foot of the see en tower and look straight up — then look around. Almost everything on this walk is within sight of where you are now, more or less in a line running north, from the lake toward the old heart of downtown.

This is the money and power walk. It starts at the tower a railway built, runs through the black towers of the banks and the hidden city underneath them, and ends at a place where Toronto keeps both of its city halls — the one it built in eighteen ninety-nine, and the one it built to replace it — standing across the street from each other, staring.

That's the thread to hold onto. Toronto has never been especially good at tearing things down. Some cities clear the past away and build clean; this one tends to keep the old version around, give it a new job, and let it sit next to whatever came after. You'll see it in a bank that became a shrine, and you'll see it most of all at the end, where the two city halls face off across Bay Street like an argument nobody won.

The route is an easy one: a fairly straight line north, mostly flat, maybe twenty minutes of walking if you don't stop — which you will. And in winter, most of the middle stretch has a warm underground twin you can duck into.

Start where the whole skyline starts. Look up at the see en tower.
