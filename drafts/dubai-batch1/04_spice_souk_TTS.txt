You'll smell this market before you can see it.

You should be on the day rah side of the creek, a short walk in from the ah brah station, where the lanes narrow and the shopfronts start spilling over — sacks rolled open at the mouth, wooden scoops standing upright in powders the colour of rust and brick and mustard. This is the Spice sook, the smallest of the old markets and the one that works hardest on your senses.

Follow your nose down the first lane. The sweet smoke underneath everything is frankincense — hardened tears of tree resin from oh mahn, just along the coast, sold by the scoop and burned at home on charcoal. Look closely at the trays: the paler and cleaner the tear, the finer the grade, and most merchants will burn a piece so you can smell the difference for yourself. That's the house style here — everything is offered to your nose first, and nobody hurries you.

Now find the black limes. They look like something that went wrong: small, dark, hollow, wrinkled as walnuts. In fact they're whole limes boiled in brine and dried in the sun, and dropped into stews and rice across the Gulf they give a sour, smoky depth that nothing else quite manages. loo mee, they're called. Buy three — they weigh almost nothing, and they'll outlast your luggage.

Around them, the rest of the inventory: rose petals by the sack, cinnamon in whole rolled bark, saffron in small tins kept behind the counter, dried chillies strung up like decorations. Nearly all of it still arrives the old way, by boat, from Iran and India and East Africa, and much of it is sold to restaurants and households before a single visitor walks in.

So here is what this little grid of lanes really is: the oldest retail idea the city owns — goods off a boat, sold within smelling distance of the water — and every enormous glass mall Dubai has built since is this sook scaled up and chilled down. The malls got the size. The sook kept the smell.

Before you go, let a merchant burn a little frankincense for you, even if you buy nothing. Some cities hand out maps. This one hands out smoke.
