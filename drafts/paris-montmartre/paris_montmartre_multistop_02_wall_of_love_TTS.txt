Step close and you'll see it's covered in the same three words, over and over, in hundreds of different hands and alphabets: I love you. Written here around three hundred times, in roughly two hundred and fifty languages.

It's not ancient — it went up in the year two thousand — and the story behind it is simple and a little obsessive. A musician named fraydereek baron spent years going around with notebooks, asking people to write I love you in their own language, collecting the phrase from strangers, from embassies, from anyone he could find. An artist, klair keetoh, arranged the whole collection onto this wall of lava stone, built to last.

Look for the scattered red shapes among the writing. They're the pieces of a broken heart — the idea being that all these separated fragments of love, in all these languages, add up to one whole.
