Nothing here matches, and that was more or less the plan.

You should be on Potsdamer Strasse, south-west of Potsdamer Platz, where the buildings stop pretending to belong together: a golden tent to the north, a black glass temple to the south, one small old church stranded between them, and a vast construction site with a long grey saddle roof in the middle of it all.

Face the gold tent first. When the Wall went up, West Berlin lost its centre — the museums, the opera, the concert halls all sat in the East — so it built a replacement cultural quarter out here, on ground already emptied by Nazi planning and then by war. The tent is the Philharmonie, finished in 1963 by Hans Scharoun, and its real revolution is inside: the orchestra sits in the middle, with the audience rising around it on terraces like a vineyard, no seat far from the music, no seat behind a pillar of privilege. Concert halls around the world have copied it ever since.

Now the black temple: the Neue Nationalgalerie, the last building by Mies van der Rohe — a single square steel roof, over a thousand tonnes of it, floating on eight slender columns above walls of nothing but glass. The roof was welded flat on the ground and jacked into the air in one day in 1967, with the elderly architect watching. It holds the modern art museum, freshly restored this decade, and it remains the most disciplined thing this untidy city owns.

The construction site between them will someday be berlin modern, a museum for the twentieth century's art — its shell and that great saddle roof are already up, though the project runs years late and far over budget, in the honoured Berlin tradition. Best not to promise it a date.

As a public square, the Kulturforum never quite worked — windswept, traffic-crossed, a cultural quarter without a city around it. But the buildings are two of the twentieth century's best, and that's very West Berlin: substance first, urbanity still on order. Catch a concert if you can. Otherwise, the gallery steps are a fine place to sit and watch the mismatch not matching.
