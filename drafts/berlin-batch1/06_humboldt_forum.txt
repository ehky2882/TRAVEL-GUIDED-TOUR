The building in front of you is a copy of a demolished palace, standing on the grave of a demolished parliament. Nothing about it is neutral — which may be the most Berlin thing about it.

You should be on Schlossplatz, facing the long baroque front of the Humboldt Forum: pale stone, rows of sculpted window frames, a green dome with a golden cross. It looks three hundred years old. It opened in 2021.

First, what stood here. For five centuries this was the Berlin Palace of the Hohenzollerns. The war left it a burnt shell, and in 1950 the East German government dynamited the ruins — a deliberate erasure of Prussia. In the 1970s the GDR built the Palast der Republik on the site: bronze glass, a parliament with a bowling alley, restaurants, a dance hall — and genuinely loved by many East Germans. After reunification it was shut for asbestos, argued over for years, and demolished in its turn. Then Germany voted to rebuild the palace facades. The erasure was erased.

Walk the building's sides and you'll find three baroque fronts reconstructed stone by stone — and a fourth, on the river, left deliberately plain and modern: the building admitting, on one face at least, what it actually is.

Now find the balcony on the portal facing the square. In 1918, Karl Liebknecht proclaimed a socialist republic from the original. When the GDR blew up the palace, it saved that one portal and set it into its State Council building, which still stands a few hundred metres south. So the real balcony lives down the road in a communist government building, and the rebuilt palace wears a replica of its own most revolutionary piece.

Inside are Berlin's non-European collections — and with them, the argument over how such things were acquired in the colonial era. Germany has begun returning objects, most famously bronzes to Nigeria. That debate isn't finished; this building is where it lives.

[beat]

Some cities argue in newspapers. Berlin argues in buildings. Keep the scars, or rebuild what was lost? The Reichstag kept its scars. This square chose resurrection — and had to demolish something to get it. Both answers are sincere. Neither is innocent.

Before you leave, walk around to the river side, where the stone goes quiet. Decide for yourself what the building is — healing or costume. The whole city is still deciding with you.
