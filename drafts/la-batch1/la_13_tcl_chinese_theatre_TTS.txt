TCL Chinese Theater

This is the most famous movie theater in the world, and it earned the title the old-fashioned way: pure, unapologetic showmanship. Sid graw muhn opened the Chinese Theater in nineteen twenty seven with the premiere of Cecil B. duh mill's The King of Kings — searchlights, crowds, stars arriving by limousine — and in doing so more or less invented the Hollywood premiere as we know it.

graw muhn was a showman first and a theater operator second. He'd already built the Egyptian Theater a few blocks east in nineteen twenty two, riding the King Tut craze; for the follow-up he went bigger — a movie palace styled as a great red pagoda, with imported temple bells, stone guardian dogs, and an interior that made going to the pictures feel like attending a coronation. None of it is remotely authentic and all of it is magnificent, which is roughly the founding formula of this entire town.

But the reason you're standing in this particular forecourt is the concrete. Nearly two hundred slabs carry the handprints, footprints, and signatures of a century of stars. The origin story says silent-film star Norma tahl mij accidentally stepped into wet cement during construction, and graw muhn, being graw muhn, saw a goldmine where anyone else would have seen a repair job. It's a story too good to check, which in Hollywood counts as true. Either way, the tradition took: the prints run from the silent era right up to whoever was honored last month, and they're the industry's oldest souvenir — fame you can measure your own hands against, which is exactly why people cross oceans to crouch on this pavement.

Two footnotes. The theater still works for a living — it's an active premiere venue with a giant eye max screen inside, and on a red-carpet night the whole block seals off, just like nineteen twenty seven. And the name: a Chinese electronics company bought the naming rights some years back, so graw muhn's Chinese is officially the T C L Chinese now. Sid, who understood better than anyone alive that attention is a thing you can sell, would probably have respected it.

Before you move on, find the prints with the smallest shoes. It's humbling out here.
