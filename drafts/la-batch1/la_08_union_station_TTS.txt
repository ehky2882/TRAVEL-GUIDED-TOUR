Union Station

Union Station is the last of the great American railway stations — and it opened in nineteen thirty nine, which means Los Angeles built its temple to the train at almost exactly the moment the city decided, permanently, on the car. That timing explains everything you're about to see.

Walk into the main hall. The ceiling floats cathedral-high above an inlaid floor built to outlast us all, enormous art deco chandeliers hang over rows of deep leather armchairs — original, and still about the most comfortable public seating in California — and light pours through windows tall as houses. The style is a blend nobody has really pulled off since: the white walls, arches, and red tile roofs of Mission Revival, streamlined with the moh dern glamour of the late nineteen thirties. People called it Mission moh dern, and it exists more or less only here. On either side of the hall, doors open onto walled garden courtyards where you can wait for a train under orange trees and birds of paradise, because this station was designed to sell every arriving passenger a very specific dream of California within their first five minutes.

The ground it stands on carries a harder story. This was the site of the city's original Chinatown, and its residents and businesses were displaced for the station's construction; the community rebuilt a short walk north, where Chinatown thrives today. It's a history worth holding quietly as you admire what replaced it.

Now, the twist that saved this place: because the freeway era arrived almost immediately, Union Station never got busy enough for anyone to bother modernizing it. No dropped ceilings, no fluorescent retrofit, no demolition to build something bigger — the neglect was the preservation. And then the story flipped again. With the Metro rail system spreading across the county, this is now the busiest rail hub in the western United States, and the building that felt almost beside the point at its own opening finally has the crowds it was drawn for.

Movie location scouts, for the record, never stopped noticing. You have seen this room before, many times. Sink into one of those leather chairs for a minute and it may start coming back to you.
