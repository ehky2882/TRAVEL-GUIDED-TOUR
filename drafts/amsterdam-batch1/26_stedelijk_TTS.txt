Amsterdammers took one look at it and called it the bathtub, and the name stuck so completely that the museum uses it too.

It sounds like nothing — but this was one of the first white-cube galleries anywhere in the world, the idea that art needs silence around it, and nearly every modern gallery you have ever walked through is quietly copying this building.

So look again at the bathtub, added in twenty twelve. It is not white by accident — its architects said the form was a tribute to sand berg, the museum wearing its own big idea on the outside. And the new wing turned the entrance around to face the square, so that a museum which once kept its back to the lawn now opens straight onto it.

Inside, it is usually the least crowded of the three big museums on this square, which is its own kind of gift. But even if you never go in, you now know the joke hiding in plain sight: a building famous for its white walls, wrapped in the whitest wall in Amsterdam.
