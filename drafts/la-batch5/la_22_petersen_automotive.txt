Every museum on this street holds still. This one looks like it's doing eighty.

You should be at Wilshire and Fairfax, on the corner opposite the Academy Museum's gold cylinder, looking at a building wrapped in ribbons — long bands of brushed stainless steel streaming around a body of hot-rod red, as if someone froze the airflow over a car and made it architecture. This is the Petersen Automotive Museum, and in Los Angeles, a museum about the car is not a niche institution. It's a museum about the landlord.

The bones underneath are older than the ribbons. Inside that red shell is a department store from 1962, designed by Welton Becket — the same firm behind the Capitol Records tower — and when magazine publisher Robert Petersen went looking for a museum home in the 1990s, he picked it precisely because it was nearly windowless: a big sealed box where sunlight couldn't fade the paint on anything. The museum opened in 1994 looking like what it was, a converted store. Then in 2015 came the wrapper: about a hundred tons of steel in more than three hundred custom sections, each one different, streaming around the box.

Critics were split — one famously called it happily tasteless — and the public voted with their cameras. Both sides have a point, which is very Los Angeles: this has always been a city where hot dog stands looked like hot dogs and buildings advertised themselves, and the Petersen simply does it at freeway scale.

Inside, the collection makes the argument the outside promises: race cars, concept cars from the era when the future had fins, machines built for the movies, and the story of how one invention laid out this entire city — the boulevards, the sprawl, the drive-ins, the parking lots. Los Angeles didn't adopt the car; they grew up together.

[beat]

So stand on this corner and take in the pairing: across the street, the movies in a gold cylinder; here, the automobile in silver ribbons. The two machines that built Los Angeles, facing each other at one intersection, each wearing its Sunday best. If you only have time for the outside, circle the block once. The building is the first exhibit.
